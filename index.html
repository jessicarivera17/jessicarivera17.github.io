Hi there, my name is Jessica Rivera and I am a 22 year old undergraduate student at University of California, Riverside. I am currently studying psychology and minoring in education. I am looking to pursue a career path that supports mental health. I graduated from Norco College last year, and received two associate degrees. The first one is for behavioral and social sciences and second, was psychology. I graduated Norco College with distinction. I enjoy making connections with new people, espceially within the realms of social development. I am currently a full-time student looking to fulfill new experiences as a behaviral health specialist. I am passionate about helping individuals who struggle with their mental health because often in culture, there is a stigma. My goal as a mental and behaviral health worker is to break this stigma and make clients feel welcomed and accepted just as they are. A huge thing that our society lacks is compassion because people of give up on each other. It is a sad reality and I want to help repair this crisis as a psychologist. Of course, it is not contigent on my career, but my intrinsic desire to make a difference in the concealed brokenness within our society. With social media, people tend to show that all is well, while others compare there lives to the perceived perfection of each photo, update, and story. This can take a toll on mental health, so its important to remind clients and patients of reality and not perceived reality. Everyone has their struggles one way or another and within my chosen career path it is not only my job, but my passion to support others who struggle with their mental health.
